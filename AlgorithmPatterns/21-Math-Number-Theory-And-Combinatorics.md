# Math, Number Theory, and Combinatorics

## 1. Why this file exists
- Some questions are mostly math, not data structure heavy.
- If you miss the math trick, no standard pattern helps enough.

## 2. Recognition signals
- GCD or divisibility conditions.
- Prime numbers or factorization.
- Modular arithmetic.
- Count combinations or permutations.
- Fast exponentiation or large power under modulo.

## 3. High-yield tools
- GCD and LCM
- Euclidean algorithm
- Sieve of Eratosthenes
- Fast power
- Modular arithmetic
- Combinations and permutations basics
- Pigeonhole or counting insight

## 4. Common question types
- Check power of two or prime. Example: [LeetCode 231 - Power of Two](https://leetcode.com/problems/power-of-two/) (Easy)
- Count primes. Example: [LeetCode 204 - Count Primes](https://leetcode.com/problems/count-primes/) (Medium)
- GCD of array patterns. Example: [LeetCode 1979 - Find Greatest Common Divisor of Array](https://leetcode.com/problems/find-greatest-common-divisor-of-array/) (Easy)
- Modular exponentiation. Examples: [LeetCode 50 - Pow(x, n)](https://leetcode.com/problems/powx-n/) (Medium), [LeetCode 372 - Super Pow](https://leetcode.com/problems/super-pow/) (Medium)
- Combinatorial counting of arrangements. Example: [LeetCode 526 - Beautiful Arrangement](https://leetcode.com/problems/beautiful-arrangement/) (Medium)
- Fraction or probability simplification. Example: [LeetCode 592 - Fraction Addition and Subtraction](https://leetcode.com/problems/fraction-addition-and-subtraction/) (Medium)

## 5. Common mistakes
- Integer overflow.
- Forgetting modulo at each step.
- Using factorials directly when inputs are large.
- Missing a simple math observation and overcomplicating the problem.

## 6. Interview answer in one line
- Math-based questions are solved by spotting the right arithmetic invariant, divisibility rule, or counting formula before reaching for heavy data structures.
